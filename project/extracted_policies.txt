[P]PART 1: GOVERNANCE & SCOPE OF SERVICES
[P]
[P]
[P]1.0 PURPOSE
[P]
[P]The purpose of this policy is to define the operational standards, scope of services, and patient care protocols for NH Care Center, LLC. These policies are designed to ensure compliance with the New Hampshire Code of Administrative Rules, Part He-P 809 (Home Health Care Providers) and RSA 151, ensuring the highest quality of care is delivered to patients in their place of residence.
[P]
[P]Terminology Note: In this manual, the terms “patient” and “client” are used interchangeably and carry the same meaning as defined in He-P 809.03(an): any person admitted to or receiving care or services from a Home Health Care Provider licensed under RSA 151 and He-P 809.
[P]
[P]
[P]2.0 NON-DISCRIMINATION STATEMENT
[P]
[P]In accordance with RSA 151:21, NH Care Center shall not deny admission, care, or services based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, or source of payment.
[P]
[P]
[P]3.0 POLICY & PROCEDURE REVIEW CYCLE
[P]
[P]In compliance with He-P 809.14(d), all policies and procedures contained in this manual shall be reviewed at least every two (2) years. Reviews shall be documented with the date of review, the name and title of the reviewer, a summary of any changes made, and the effective date of the revised policy. A Revision History Log shall be maintained as an appendix to this manual.
[P]
[P]
[P]4.0 POSTING REQUIREMENTS AT LICENSED PREMISES
[P]
[P]In accordance with RSA 151:29 and He-P 809.14, NH Care Center shall post the following documents in a conspicuous public area at the licensed premises (143 North Main St, STE 202 A, Concord, NH 03301):
[P]
[P]
[P]The current license certificate issued in accordance with RSA 151:2.
[P]
[P]
[P]All inspection reports issued for the previous 12 months.
[P]
[P]
[P]A copy of the Home Care Clients’ Bill of Rights as specified by RSA 151:21-b.
[P]
[P]
[P]A copy of the agency’s complaint procedure, including a statement that complaints may be submitted in writing to: NH DHHS, Health Facilities Administration, 129 Pleasant Street, Concord, NH 03301, or by calling 1-800-852-3345 (Ext. 9039).
[P]
[P]
[P]A copy of the agency’s policies and procedures relative to the implementation of patient rights and responsibilities.
[P]
[P]
[P]The agency’s plan for fire safety, evacuation, and emergencies, identifying the location of and access to all fire exits.
[P]
[P]
[P]5.0 FIREARMS SAFETY POLICY
[P]
[P]In compliance with He-P 809.14(b)(2), NH Care Center maintains this policy to ensure the safety of all persons present where firearms are permitted.
[P]
[P]
[P]Licensed Premises: Firearms are not permitted in the agency’s office unless secured in a locked container inaccessible to visitors and staff during business hours.
[P]
[P]
[P]Patient Homes: Staff entering a patient’s home where firearms are present shall ensure firearms are stored safely and not in the immediate care area. If a firearm is unsecured and poses a safety risk, the caregiver shall pause services, move to a safe location, and notify the Administrator immediately.
[P]
[P]
[P]Documentation: The presence of firearms in a patient’s home shall be noted in the Home Environment Safety Assessment and communicated to all staff assigned to that patient.
[P]
[P]
[P]Training: All staff shall receive orientation on this policy during initial onboarding.
[P]
[P]
[P]6.0 PROHIBITION ON FALSIFICATION OF RECORDS
[P]
[P]In compliance with He-P 809.14(f), no employee, contractor, or agent of NH Care Center, LLC shall falsify any documentation or provide false or misleading information to the New Hampshire Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) or any other regulatory authority. Violations of this policy shall result in immediate disciplinary action up to and including termination and may result in enforcement action by the department.
[P]
[P]
[P]7.0 CONTRACTED AND ARRANGED SERVICES
[P]
[P]In accordance with He-P 809.15(a)-(b), NH Care Center shall maintain written contractual agreements for all services provided by arrangement with other entities. Each contractual agreement shall:
[P]
[P]
[P]Identify the specific care and services to be provided.
[P]
[P]
[P]Specify the qualifications of the personnel providing the care and services.
[P]
[P]
[P]Require that the HHCP must authorize the services prior to delivery.
[P]
[P]
[P]Stipulate that NH Care Center retains professional responsibility for all care and services provided under the arrangement.
[P]
[P]
[P]8.0 PERSONAL CARE & SUPPORT SERVICES (NON-MEDICAL)
[P]
[P]Provided by Home Health Aides (HHA), Personal Care Aides (PCA), or Licensed Nursing Assistants (LNA) under RN supervision.
[P]
[P]
[P]8.1 Supervision of Support Staff (He-P 809.17 Compliance)
[P]
[P]
[P]Skilled Cases: When a patient receives skilled nursing/therapy, an RN must make a supervisory visit to the home at least every 14 days to assess the aide’s performance.
[P]
[P]
[P]Non-Skilled Cases: When a patient receives only personal care, an RN must make a supervisory visit at least every 60 days to ensure the Plan of Care is being followed.
[P]
[P]
[P]8.2 In-Home Personal Hygiene & Grooming
[P]
[P]Definition: Assistance with Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) related to self-care performed in the patient’s home.
[P]
[P]Scope:
[P]
[P]
[P]Bathing: Assistance with bed bath, sponge bath, tub, or shower.
[P]
[P]
[P]Oral Hygiene: Brushing teeth and denture care.
[P]
[P]
[P]Grooming: Hair care, shaving (electric razor only), and nail care (limited to filing/cleaning; no cutting for diabetic patients or those on blood thinners).
[P]
[P]
[P]Dressing: Assistance with dressing and undressing.
[P]
[P]
[P]Toileting: Assistance with perineal care, incontinence care (briefs/pads), and ostomy bag emptying (not changing the wafer/flange). Strictly Prohibited: Aides shall not perform digital stimulation or insert suppositories/enemas.
[P]
[P]
[P]8.3 Household Support (Homemaker Services)
[P]
[P]Definition: Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (IADLs) aimed at maintaining a safe and clean living environment within the patient’s residence.
[P]
[P]
[P]Light Housekeeping: Dusting, vacuuming main living areas, and cleaning the patient’s bathroom and kitchen.
[P]
[P]
[P]Laundry: Washing, drying, and folding the patient’s personal clothing and bed linens.
[P]
[P]
[P]Meal Services: Meal planning, preparation, and assistance with feeding (only if no swallowing difficulties/dysphagia are present).
[P]
[P]
[P]Errands: Grocery shopping and errand running within the local community.
[P]
[P]
[P]8.4 Companionship
[P]
[P]Definition: Emotional support and socialization to prevent isolation and ensure safety.
[P]
[P]
[P]Socialization: Engaging in conversation, games, reading, or hobbies.
[P]
[P]
[P]Escort: Accompanying patients to medical appointments or social outings.
[P]
[P]
[P]Safety Supervision: General supervision to ensure safety (e.g., wandering prevention for patients with dementia/cognitive impairment).
[P]
[P]
[P]8.5 Mobility Support
[P]
[P]Definition: Physical assistance to promote safe movement and prevent falls within the home.
[P]
[P]
[P]Transfers: Assistance with transfers (bed to chair, chair to toilet) using appropriate techniques (e.g., gait belt). Note: Mechanical lifts (Hoyer) require specific staff training and documented competency.
[P]
[P]
[P]Ambulation: Walking supervision and standby assistance.
[P]
[P]
[P]Range of Motion: Passive or active Range of Motion (ROM) exercises only as prescribed by a therapist/physician and specifically delegated by the RN in the Plan of Care.
[P]
[P]
[P]8.6 In-Home Respite Care
[P]
[P]Definition: Temporary short-term care provided to relieve the primary family caregiver. Includes a combination of any of the above services (hygiene, companionship, safety supervision) provided for a designated block of time specifically to allow the family caregiver rest.
[P]
[P]
[P]8.7 Delegation of Nursing Tasks and Medication Administration
[P]
[P]In accordance with He-P 809.16 and the New Hampshire Nurse Practice Act (Board of Nursing rules), a Registered Nurse (RN) may delegate specific nursing tasks (such as medication administration or other specialized tasks not typically within the baseline scope of an LNA, HHA, or PCA) only if strict protocols are followed.
[P]
[P]
[P]Documented Training: The delegating RN must provide specific, documented training to the unlicensed caregiver on how to perform the exact task for the specific patient. This training must include the purpose of the task, the steps involved, potential complications, and when to notify the RN.
[P]
[P]
[P]Competency Evaluation: The RN must directly observe the caregiver performing the task safely and document this competency verification in the personnel/clinical record.
[P]
[P]
[P]Specific Written Delegation: The delegated task must be explicitly detailed in the written Aide Plan of Care.
[P]
[P]Permissible Delegated Tasks (Examples):
[P]
[P]
[P]Administration of routine oral medications from a pre-filled compliance aid (e.g., pillbox) set up by a nurse or pharmacist.
[P]
[P]
[P]Assistance with the application of prescribed topical medications (creams/ointments) to intact skin.
[P]
[P]
[P]Routine ostomy care (emptying the pouch).
[P]
[P]
[P]Obtaining vital signs.
[P]
[P]Prohibited Tasks (Non-Delegable):
[P]
[P]
[P]Tasks requiring nursing assessment, judgment, or complex clinical decision-making.
[P]
[P]
[P]Administration of injectable medications (e.g., insulin), IV medications, or medications via feeding tube.
[P]
[P]
[P]Sterile procedures, including sterile dressing changes.
[P]
[P]
[P]Initial assessment or evaluation of a patient’s health status.
[P]
[P]
[P]Creating or altering the Plan of Care.
[P]
[P]
[P]RN Signature: The specific delegation, training record, and competency sign-off must be signed and dated by the delegating Registered Nurse.
[P]
[P]
[P]Patient-Specific: Delegation is strictly specific to the individual patient and the individual caregiver. It cannot be transferred to another caregiver or applied to another patient without a new training and competency evaluation process.
[P]
[P]
[P]9.0 SKILLED NURSING SERVICES (RN/LPN)
[P]
[P]Provided by licensed nurses in accordance with the NH Nurse Practice Act.
[P]
[P]
[P]9.1 Nursing Assessments & Recertification
[P]
[P]
[P]Initial Assessment: Must be completed by an RN within 48 hours of referral or on the physician-ordered Start of Care date.
[P]
[P]
[P]Recertification: A comprehensive reassessment must be performed every 60 days to update the Plan of Care (POC) and verify continued medical necessity.
[P]
[P]
[P]9.2 Medication Management (He-P 809.16)
[P]
[P]
[P]Administration: Nurses may administer medications (IV, IM, SQ, Oral) only with a valid, signed physician’s order.
[P]
[P]
[P]Medication Pours/Setup: Nurses may fill pillboxes for up to 14 days in advance, provided the storage container is labeled with the patient’s name and medication details.
[P]
[P]
[P]Controlled Substances: Must be counted at every visit if administered by agency staff.
[P]
[P]
[P]PRN Medications: Pro re nata (as needed) medications shall only be administered with a valid order that specifies the indication, dosage, frequency limit, and maximum daily dose. The nurse shall document the reason for administration, the time administered, and the patient’s response.
[P]
[P]
[P]Over-the-Counter (OTC) Medications: OTC medications shall not be administered without a physician’s order or explicit inclusion in the Plan of Care. Any patient-initiated OTC use shall be documented and reported to the prescribing physician.
[P]
[P]
[P]Medication Administration Documentation: Each medication administration event shall be documented in the clinical record, including: drug name, dose, route, time of administration, and the patient’s response or any adverse reactions.
[P]
[P]
[P]9.3 Advanced Clinical Procedures
[P]
[P]
[P]Wound Care: Staging, measurement, and dressing changes per specific orders.
[P]
[P]
[P]IV Therapy: PICC/Midline care, port access, and infusion therapy (antibiotics, hydration, TPN).
[P]
[P]
[P]Catheter Care: Insertion and maintenance of foley/suprapubic catheters.
[P]
[P]
[P]9.4 Tube Feeding Management (Enteral Nutrition)
[P]
[P]Policy: Enteral nutrition shall be administered by a licensed nurse in accordance with a specific physician’s order regarding formula, rate, volume, and frequency.
[P]
[P]
[P]Site Care: Cleaning, assessment, and dressing changes of Gastrostomy (PEG) or Jejunostomy (J-tube) sites to monitor for infection or skin breakdown.
[P]
[P]
[P]Patency & Residuals: Verifying tube patency and checking gastric residual volumes (GRV) prior to feedings if ordered by the physician.
[P]
[P]
[P]Administration: Delivery of bolus, intermittent, or continuous feedings via pump or gravity, and water flushes for hydration and medication delivery.
[P]
[P]
[P]9.5 General Health & Chronic Disease Monitoring
[P]
[P]Policy: Skilled nurses shall provide observation, assessment, and teaching to manage chronic conditions and prevent exacerbations requiring hospitalization.
[P]
[P]
[P]Vital Signs: Monitoring blood pressure, heart rate, respiratory rate, temperature, and oxygen saturation (SpO2).
[P]
[P]
[P]Disease Management & Education: Instruction on self-management strategies for conditions including but not limited to Diabetes (foot care/insulin), Heart Failure (daily weights/edema check), and COPD (pursed-lip breathing/O2 safety).
[P]
[P]
[P]Care Coordination: Prompt communication with the primary care provider regarding critical values or negative trends in the patient’s condition.

[P]PART 5: ADMISSION CRITERIA & ONBOARDING PROCEDURES
[P]
[P]
[P]1.0 ADMISSION CRITERIA
[P]
[P]NH Care Center shall accept patients who:
[P]
[P]
[P]Reside within the defined service area (New Hampshire).
[P]
[P]
[P]Have needs that can be safely met in the home environment.
[P]
[P]
[P]Agree to the Terms of Service and Safety Policies.
[P]
[P]
[P]1.1 Service Determination (Intake Triage)
[P]
[P]Upon initial contact, the Intake Coordinator shall identify the primary needs of the patient to determine the appropriate service track and regulatory requirements:
[P]
[P]Skilled Nursing Track:
[P]
[P]
[P]Definition: Patient requires services that can only be safely performed by or under the supervision of a licensed nurse (RN/LPN) or therapist. Examples: Wound care, IV therapy, IM injections, complex medication teaching/management, unstable vital signs.
[P]
[P]
[P]Requirement: A written or verbal Physician’s Order is MANDATORY before care can begin.
[P]
[P]Non-Medical (Personal Care/Support) Track:
[P]
[P]
[P]Definition: Patient is medically stable but requires assistance with Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) or Instrumental ADLs (IADLs). Examples: Bathing, dressing, meal preparation, light housekeeping.
[P]
[P]
[P]Requirement: Services are typically directed by the patient/family. A Physician’s Order is generally not required (unless mandated by a specific payer like Medicaid Waiver), but an RN Assessment and Care Plan are still required by Agency Policy.
[P]
[P]
[P]2.0 PROCEDURE: SKILLED NURSING ONBOARDING WORKFLOW
[P]
[P]This workflow applies when a patient requires Licensed Nursing (RN/LPN) services under a Physician’s Order.
[P]
[P]Step 1: Referral & Intake
[P]
[P]
[P]Action: Receive referral (face sheet, H&P, discharge summary) from hospital, physician, or facility.
[P]
[P]
[P]Verification: Intake Coordinator verifies insurance eligibility and ensures the specific medical needs (e.g., wound vac, IV) are within the agency’s scope and staffing capability.
[P]
[P]Step 2: Physician Orders (He-P 809.15)
[P]
[P]
[P]Requirement: Written or verbal orders must be obtained from the ordering physician prior to the start of care.
[P]
[P]
[P]Content: Orders must specify treatments, medication, diet, and activity level.
[P]
[P]Step 3: Initial RN Assessment (The “Start of Care”) (He-P 809.15)
[P]
[P]
[P]Timing: An RN must perform the initial comprehensive assessment within 48 hours of the referral or on the physician-ordered Start of Care (SOC) date.
[P]
[P]Comprehensive Clinical Assessment Scope (Head-to-Toe):
[P]
[P]
[P]Neurological: Level of consciousness, orientation (Person, Place, Time), memory recall, pupil reaction, and grip strength/equality.
[P]
[P]
[P]HEENT (Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose, Throat): Vision/hearing deficits, condition of oral mucosa (hydration, sores), and swallowing ability (dysphagia screen).
[P]
[P]
[P]Cardiopulmonary: Auscultation of heart sounds (regularity/murmurs) and lung sounds (clear/crackles/wheezes) in all lobes; assessment of O2 saturation, capillary refill, and peripheral edema (pitting vs. non-pitting).
[P]
[P]
[P]Gastrointestinal (GI): Abdominal auscultation (bowel sounds), palpation for tenderness/distension, and review of bowel habits (last BM, constipation/diarrhea protocols).
[P]
[P]
[P]Genitourinary (GU): Assessment of urinary continence, characteristics of urine (color/odor), and management of devices (Foley/Suprapubic catheters).
[P]
[P]
[P]Integumentary (Skin): Complete full-body skin check using the Braden Scale to risk-stratify for pressure ulcers. Detailed documentation of any existing wounds, incisions, or bruises (location, size, drainage).
[P]
[P]
[P]Musculoskeletal: Evaluation of Range of Motion (ROM), gait stability, use of assistive devices, and fall risk using a validated tool (e.g., MAHC-10 or Morse Fall Scale).
[P]
[P]
[P]Pain Assessment: Evaluation of pain intensity (0-10 scale), location, frequency, aggravating/alleviating factors, and effectiveness of current pain management interventions.
[P]
[P]
[P]Medication Reconciliation: Review of all prescription and OTC medications against the hospital discharge list and physician orders to identify drug-drug interactions, duplications, or compliance issues.
[P]
[P]
[P]Homebound Verification: Documentation confirming the patient meets the “Homebound” criteria (leaving home requires a taxing effort and is infrequent) if applicable for insurance billing.
[P]
[P]Step 4: Mandatory Disclosures (RSA 151)
[P]
[P]
[P]Patient Rights: A written copy of the Home Care Clients’ Bill of Rights (RSA 151:21-b).
[P]
[P]
[P]Advance Directives: Ask if the patient has a Living Will/DNR and obtain a copy.
[P]
[P]
[P]Financial Liability: Written notice of expected costs and insurance coverage.
[P]
[P]
[P]Complaint Procedure: A written copy of the agency’s complaint procedure, including DHHS contact information.
[P]
[P]Step 5: Plan of Care (POC) Authorization
[P]
[P]
[P]The RN develops the POC based on assessment findings.
[P]
[P]
[P]The POC is sent to the attending physician for signature. Verbal orders may be used to start care but must be countersigned.
[P]
[P]
[P]3.0 PROCEDURE: NON-MEDICAL (PERSONAL CARE) ONBOARDING WORKFLOW
[P]
[P]This workflow applies when a patient requires only Personal Care (ADL) or Homemaker services, typically Self-Pay or Medicaid Waiver.
[P]
[P]Step 1: Inquiry & Screening
[P]
[P]
[P]Receive inquiry from patient or family.
[P]
[P]
[P]Conduct phone screening to determine if needs are strictly non-medical. If medical needs (e.g., insulin injections) are identified, the workflow defaults to Skilled Nursing.
[P]
[P]Step 2: Initial RN Assessment (He-P 809.15)
[P]
[P]
[P]Even for non-medical personal care, a Registered Nurse must conduct the initial assessment to evaluate the patient’s functional status and safety.
[P]
[P]
[P]Timing: Must occur prior to or on the date services are to be provided.
[P]
[P]Functional & Safety Assessment Scope:
[P]
[P]
[P]ADL Capabilities: Evaluation of the patient’s ability to perform specific tasks to determine the exact level of assistance needed.
[P]
[P]
[P]Cognitive Status: Assessment of orientation (Person, Place, Time) and safety judgment.
[P]
[P]
[P]Fall Risk Assessment: Evaluation of gait, balance, history of falls, and use of assistive devices.
[P]
[P]
[P]Home Environment Safety: Inspection of the home for hazards, including loose rugs, poor lighting, lack of bathroom grab bars, smoke detector functionality, and presence of firearms.
[P]
[P]
[P]Emergency Preparedness: Assignment of a Triage Level (1, 2, or 3) based on the patient’s dependence on care during a natural disaster.
[P]
[P]
[P]Skin Integrity Check: Visual inspection of skin to report any redness or bruising.
[P]
[P]Step 3: Service Agreement & Financial Consent
[P]
[P]
[P]Establish a written contract detailing the specific hourly rates, minimum hours, and cancellation policy.
[P]
[P]
[P]As required by RSA 151, the patient must be informed in writing of their financial responsibility before care begins.
[P]
[P]Step 4: Aide Plan of Care (Task List)
[P]
[P]
[P]The RN creates a written instruction sheet (Aide Care Plan) detailing specific ADL tasks.
[P]
[P]
[P]The RN delegates these tasks to the HHA/LNA/PCA.
[P]
[P]Step 5: Orientation & Start of Care
[P]
[P]
[P]The RN or Agency Representative orients the assigned caregiver to the Plan of Care.
[P]
[P]
[P]The RN schedules the first supervisory visit (required within 60 days for non-skilled cases).

[P]1.0 DEVELOPMENT & REQUIREMENTS (He-P 809.15(o)-(q))

[P]
[P]
[P]Interventions: Specific nursing actions (e.g., “Change dressing QD with wet-to-dry saline”).
[P]
[P]
[P]Frequency/Duration: Exact schedule (e.g., “SN 3w1, 2w2, 1w3”).
[P]
[P]
[P]Medications: Complete list including dose, route, and frequency.
[P]
[P]
[P]Safety Measures: Fall precautions, bleeding precautions, etc.
[P]
[P]
[P]Functional Limitations: Amputation, paralysis, dyspnea, etc.
[P]
[P]
[P]Rehabilitation Potential: Evaluation of likelihood to return to prior level of function.
[P]

[P]

[P]PART 7: INFECTION CONTROL & SAFETY (He-P 809.19)
[P]
[P]
[P]1.0 STANDARD PRECAUTIONS (UNIVERSAL PRECAUTIONS)
[P]
[P]Policy: NH Care Center considers all blood, body fluids, secretions (except sweat), non-intact skin, and mucous membranes as potentially infectious. Standard Precautions shall be applied to all patient interactions, regardless of diagnosis.
[P]
[P]
[P]1.1 Hand Hygiene
[P]
[P]Hand hygiene is the single most important measure to prevent the spread of infection.
[P]
[P]
[P]Method 1 (Alcohol-Based Hand Rub - ABHR): Preferred method for routine hand antisepsis. Apply product to palm and rub hands together, covering all surfaces until dry (approx. 20 seconds).
[P]
[P]
[P]Method 2 (Soap and Water): Required when hands are visibly soiled, after caring for a patient with C. difficile or Norovirus, and after restroom use. Scrub for at least 20 seconds.
[P]
[P]The “5 Moments” for Hand Hygiene:
[P]
[P]
[P]Before touching a patient.
[P]
[P]
[P]Before clean/aseptic procedures.
[P]
[P]
[P]After body fluid exposure risk.
[P]
[P]
[P]After touching a patient.
[P]
[P]
[P]After touching patient surroundings.
[P]
[P]
[P]1.2 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
[P]
[P]Staff must conduct a “Point of Care Risk Assessment” prior to every interaction to select appropriate PPE.
[P]
[P]
[P]Gloves: Wear for contact with blood, body fluids, mucous membranes, non-intact skin, or contaminated items. Change gloves between tasks on the same patient (dirty to clean). Never wash or reuse gloves.
[P]
[P]
[P]Gowns: Wear to protect skin/clothing during procedures likely to generate splashes or sprays of blood/body fluids.
[P]
[P]
[P]Masks/Eye Protection: Wear a surgical mask and eye protection (goggles/face shield) during procedures likely to generate splashes or sprays of respiratory secretions.
[P]
[P]
[P]2.0 TRANSMISSION-BASED PRECAUTIONS
[P]
[P]
[P]2.1 Contact Precautions
[P]
[P]
[P]Indications: MRSA (in open wound), VRE, C. difficile, Scabies, Uncontained drainage.
[P]
[P]
[P]Protocol: Gown and Gloves upon entry to the room. Dedicated equipment left in the home.
[P]
[P]
[P]2.2 Droplet Precautions
[P]
[P]
[P]Indications: Influenza, Pertussis, Mumps.
[P]
[P]
[P]Protocol: Surgical mask upon entry to the home (or within 3 feet of patient). Instruct patient to follow respiratory hygiene.
[P]
[P]
[P]2.3 Airborne Precautions
[P]
[P]
[P]Indications: Active Tuberculosis (TB), Measles, Chickenpox.
[P]
[P]
[P]Policy: NH Care Center does not generally accept patients with active airborne disease unless staff are Fit-Tested for N95 respirators. If a patient is suspected of having active TB, staff must withdraw, notify the Administrator, and refer the patient to a facility with negative pressure capability or a specialized agency.
[P]
[P]
[P]3.0 BAG TECHNIQUE
[P]
[P]
[P]Place the bag on a hard, clean, dry surface (table or chair). Never on the floor.
[P]
[P]
[P]Always place a disposable barrier (pad/newspaper/plastic) under the bag.
[P]
[P]
[P]Perform hand hygiene before opening the bag.
[P]
[P]
[P]Clean/Dirty Zones: The inside of the bag is a “Clean Zone.” Only clean hands and clean supplies enter. Used equipment/waste never goes back into the bag.
[P]
[P]
[P]Close the bag immediately after removing supplies.
[P]
[P]
[P]4.0 CLEANING & DISINFECTION OF PATIENT-CARE EQUIPMENT
[P]
[P]
[P]Reusable Equipment: Non-critical items (BP cuffs, stethoscopes, pulse oximeters) must be cleaned with an EPA-registered disinfectant wipe between every patient.
[P]
[P]
[P]Shared Equipment: Items that cannot be cleaned should be dedicated to a single patient or disposable versions used.
[P]
[P]
[P]5.0 COMMUNICABLE DISEASE & EMPLOYEE HEALTH
[P]
[P]
[P]5.1 Reporting Requirements (He-P 809.19)
[P]
[P]The Administrator shall report any outbreak of communicable disease (as defined by NH RSA 141-C) among staff or patients to the NH Division of Public Health Services within 24 hours.
[P]
[P]
[P]5.2 Staff Illness
[P]
[P]Staff with the following symptoms must not provide direct patient care:
[P]
[P]
[P]Fever (>100.4°F).
[P]
[P]
[P]Active vomiting or diarrhea.
[P]
[P]
[P]Productive cough with fever.
[P]
[P]
[P]Draining skin lesions that cannot be covered.
[P]
[P]
[P]5.3 Post-Exposure Plan (Needlesticks)
[P]
[P]In the event of a percutaneous injury (needlestick) or mucosal exposure to blood:
[P]
[P]
[P]Immediate Care: Wash the area with soap/water. Flush splashes to nose/mouth/skin with water. Irrigate eyes with clean water/saline.
[P]
[P]
[P]Notification: Notify the Administrator immediately.
[P]
[P]
[P]Medical Evaluation: The employee must seek medical evaluation within 2 hours for risk assessment and potential Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP).
[P]
[P]
[P]6.0 BIOMEDICAL WASTE MANAGEMENT
[P]
[P]
[P]Sharps: All needles, lancets, and retractable blades must be disposed of immediately in a rigid, puncture-resistant, leak-proof “Sharps” container. Recapping needles is prohibited.
[P]
[P]
[P]Liquid Waste: Pourable blood/body fluids may be carefully poured down the patient’s toilet.
[P]
[P]
[P]Solid Waste: Dressings saturated with blood (dripping) must be double-bagged and disposed of in accordance with NH solid waste rules. Non-saturated items are general household trash.

[P]PART 8: EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS (He-P 809.20)
[P]
[P]
[P]1.0 COMPREHENSIVE EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT PLAN
[P]
[P]NH Care Center maintains a written Emergency Preparedness Plan (EPP) to ensure the continuity of essential services during natural or man-made disasters (e.g., ice storms, flooding, pandemics).
[P]
[P]
[P]1.1 Hazard Vulnerability Analysis (HVA)
[P]
[P]The agency conducts an annual HVA to identify the specific risks most likely to impact the service area (e.g., in New Hampshire: Winter Storms, Power Outages, Flooding).
[P]
[P]
[P]1.2 Patient Triage System
[P]
[P]Upon admission, every patient is assigned a priority code based on their resilience during a service interruption:
[P]
[P]
[P]Level 1 (Critical/Life Sustaining): Patient cannot survive 24 hours without care (e.g., IV life-sustaining meds, insulin-dependent with no able caregiver, bedbound requiring frequent turning). Action: Must be evacuated or visited first.
[P]
[P]
[P]Level 2 (Moderate/Health Risk): Health may decline, but is not immediately life-threatening if a visit is missed for 24-48 hours.
[P]
[P]
[P]Level 3 (Low/Non-Urgent): Routine care (e.g., weekly homemaking, monthly catheter change); can safely wait 72+ hours.
[P]
[P]
[P]1.3 Notification of Local EMS and Emergency Management
[P]
[P]In accordance with He-P 809.20, NH Care Center shall coordinate with local emergency responders to ensure the safety of vulnerable individuals during an emergency.
[P]
[P]
[P]EMS Notification: With the patient’s consent, the Administrator or Clinical Director shall notify local Emergency Medical Services (EMS), fire departments, and/or emergency management officials of the location of any highly vulnerable patient (Level 1) who may require their assistance during an emergency or disaster.
[P]
[P]
[P]Life-Sustaining Equipment: Specific notification shall be provided for patients reliant on life-sustaining medical equipment requiring electricity (e.g., oxygen concentrators, ventilators) to ensure they are prioritized for wellness checks, rescue, or alternative power support during prolonged outages.
[P]
[P]
[P]2.0 DISASTER RESPONSE PROTOCOLS
[P]
[P]
[P]2.1 Activation & Command Center
[P]
[P]
[P]Administrator Responsibility: Activates the Command Center and initiates the staff calling tree.
[P]
[P]
[P]Communication: Staff attempt to contact all Level 1 patients to ensure they have supplies/power or have evacuated.
[P]
[P]
[P]Documentation: All attempts to contact patients and the outcome of those calls must be documented in the clinical record.
[P]
[P]
[P]2.2 Interruption of Services
[P]
[P]If staff cannot reach the patient’s home due to safety hazards (e.g., road closures, downed lines):
[P]
[P]
[P]The Administrator must be notified immediately.
[P]
[P]
[P]The agency must contact the patient’s emergency contact or family.
[P]
[P]
[P]If the patient is Level 1 and cannot be reached, the agency must contact local emergency services (911/Police/Fire) to request a Welfare Check.
[P]
[P]
[P]2.3 Recovery Phase
[P]
[P]Once the immediate threat passes, the agency prioritizes visits to restore normal operations, starting with Level 1 patients who remained at home.
[P]
[P]
[P]3.0 TRAINING & DRILLS
[P]
[P]
[P]Orientation: All new staff are trained on the EPP during orientation.
[P]
[P]
[P]Annual Drill: The agency conducts at least one emergency preparedness drill or table-top exercise annually to test the communication plan.

[P]PART 14: MANDATORY REPORTING OF ABUSE, NEGLECT, AND EXPLOITATION
[P]
[P]
[P]1.0 POLICY STATEMENT (RSA 161-F & RSA 169-C)
[P]
[P]In strict compliance with New Hampshire law, all employees, contractors, and volunteers of NH Care Center, LLC act as mandated reporters. Any caregiver or staff member who has a “reason to believe” (for vulnerable adults/elderly per RSA 161-F:46) or a “reason to suspect” (for children per RSA 169-C:29) that a patient has been subjected to abuse, neglect, self-neglect, or exploitation is legally mandated to report this directly to the appropriate state authorities. This legal duty rests solely with the individual who has the suspicion and cannot be delegated to a supervisor or the Administrator.
[P]
[P]
[P]2.0 STAFF TRAINING CURRICULUM
[P]
[P]Pursuant to He-P 809.17 and state law, all caregivers shall receive comprehensive training during initial orientation (prior to any direct patient contact), and annually thereafter, concerning mandatory reporting. Training shall include:
[P]
[P]
[P]Module 1 — Definitions and Signs: Physical Abuse (unexplained bruises, grip marks, burns, frequent unexplained falls); Emotional/Psychological Abuse (threats, intimidation, isolation, sudden fearfulness); Sexual Abuse (non-consensual sexual contact); Neglect (soiled clothing, malnutrition, untreated bedsores); Self-Neglect (hoarding, unsanitary conditions, refusing essential medical care); Financial Exploitation (missing personal items, unpaid bills, unauthorized credit card use).
[P]
[P]
[P]Module 2 — The “Reason to Suspect/Believe” Standard: Caregivers do not need definitive proof, a confession, or an investigation to report. They only need a reasonable suspicion based on objective observations, patient statements, or environmental indicators.
[P]
[P]
[P]Module 3 — Reporting Protocol: Exact step-by-step reporting procedure (detailed in Section 3.0 below), including information gathering and specific agency phone numbers.
[P]
[P]
[P]Module 4 — The Non-Delegation Rule: Informing the Administrator or a nursing supervisor does not fulfill the legal obligation. The individual must personally initiate the report to the state.
[P]
[P]
[P]Module 5 — Legal Protections and Penalties: Legal immunity for good-faith reports, protection against employer retaliation, and criminal penalties (misdemeanor) for failing to report.
[P]
[P]
[P]3.0 PROCEDURE FOR REPORTING A SUSPICION
[P]
[P]Step 1: Ensure Immediate Safety
[P]
[P]If the patient is in immediate, life-threatening danger, or a crime is actively in progress, the caregiver must secure their own safety and call 911 first.
[P]
[P]Step 2: Gather Required Information
[P]
[P]Before calling the state agency, gather: the patient’s name, age/date of birth, address, the nature of the suspected abuse/neglect, any evidence of previous abuse, the name of the suspected perpetrator (if known), and any other helpful information. Do NOT delay reporting if some information is missing.
[P]
[P]Step 3: Report Directly to the State
[P]
[P]For Adults/Elderly (Age 18+ — RSA 161-F):
[P]
[P]
[P]Bureau of Elderly and Adult Services (BEAS) Central Intake: 1-800-949-0470 or (603) 271-7014
[P]
[P]
[P]Address: NH DHHS — BEAS, 129 Pleasant Street, Concord, NH 03301
[P]
[P]
[P]Hours: Monday-Friday, 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM
[P]
[P]
[P]After Hours: Reports must be made immediately to local police dispatch or 911.
[P]
[P]For Children (Under Age 18 — RSA 169-C):
[P]
[P]
[P]Division for Children, Youth and Families (DCYF) Central Intake: 1-800-894-5533 or (603) 271-6562
[P]
[P]
[P]Address: NH DHHS — DCYF Central Intake, 129 Pleasant Street, Concord, NH 03301
[P]
[P]
[P]Hours: Monday-Friday, 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM
[P]
[P]
[P]After Hours: Reports must be made immediately to local police dispatch or 911.
[P]
[P]Step 4: Internal Notification (Post-State Report)
[P]
[P]Immediately after notifying BEAS, DCYF, or local law enforcement, notify the Agency Administrator that a report was filed.
[P]
[P]Exception: If the suspicion involves the Agency Administrator as the perpetrator or an accomplice, do NOT notify the Administrator internally. Report directly to the state agencies and/or law enforcement only.
[P]
[P]Step 5: Objective Documentation
[P]
[P]Document objective findings (exact quotes, descriptions of bruises, environmental hazards) in the clinical record. Do not conduct internal investigations, interrogate the patient or family, or confront the suspected abuser.
[P]
[P]
[P]4.0 PROTECTION FROM RETALIATION
[P]
[P]NH Care Center, LLC strictly prohibits any form of retaliation, discrimination, or adverse employment action against any employee who makes a good-faith report of suspected abuse, neglect, or exploitation to state authorities, in accordance with state whistleblower protections. Under RSA 161-F:47 and RSA 169-C:31, anyone participating in making a report in good faith is immune from civil or criminal liability.
[P]
